How to check for unclaimed money in your name

This is not fun. It is paperwork-adjacent. It can also be money that already belongs to you.

What “unclaimed money” actually means

Unclaimed money usually comes from things like forgotten bank accounts, old security deposits, refunds, or payments that couldn’t be delivered.

States are required to hold this money and return it if you can prove it’s yours. No prizes. No giveaways. Just a slow, official process.

Where to check safely

  • Your state’s official unclaimed property website
  • Multi-state databases run by state agencies
  • Direct links from government domains

You should never have to pay just to search.

Optional tools that may help

If you’re tracking multiple claims or deadlines, these tools can help keep things organized. Sponsored links are labeled.

  • Claim tracking spreadsheet tool
  • Identity document storage service

What to ignore

Ignore ads that promise to “unlock” money for a fee. Ignore pressure tactics. Ignore countdown timers.

Official sources

  • State unclaimed property office
  • National Association of Unclaimed Property Administrators
