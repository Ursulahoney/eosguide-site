You visited Ideal Image's website to maybe book a laser hair removal consultation. They used tracking technology to share your personal information with third parties without asking. Now there's a $3.5 million settlement and you may be owed up to $17.

What happened

Class period: January 1, 2023 – January 26, 2026

Ideal Image, the laser hair removal and aesthetics company, allegedly used tracking technologies on its website — think Meta Pixel, Google tags, and similar tools — to quietly share users' personally identifiable information with third parties. No consent asked. No opt-in. Just your data, quietly heading out the door while you were trying to book a consultation.

The lawsuit alleges this violated three separate laws: the Florida Security of Communications Act, the California Invasion of Privacy Act, and the federal Electronic Communications Privacy Act. Ideal Image denies any wrongdoing but agreed to a $3.5 million settlement to resolve the case. The settlement also requires Ideal Image to suspend the use of tracking technologies that share users' personal information without consent going forward — so at least something changes.

How to file

  5. Mail paper claims to: Gayle Minano v. Ideal Image Corporation, c/o Kroll Settlement Administration LLC, P.O. Box 225391, New York, NY 10150-5391

FAQ

Do I need proof that I visited the website?
No proof is required. You simply confirm on the claim form that you used idealimage.com to schedule a consultation between January 1, 2023 and January 26, 2026.
How much will I actually receive?
Up to $17.00. The exact amount depends on how many valid claims are filed. The total fund is $3.5 million — but attorneys' fees (up to one-third) and administration costs come out first, and the rest is split among valid claimants.
How will I get paid?
By check or electronic payment — you choose when you file. If you get a check, it expires 180 days after issuance, so cash it promptly.
Do I have to attend the hearing?
No. The June 17, 2026 hearing is via Zoom and attendance is optional. You only need to show up if you want to object in person.
What happens if I do nothing?
You get no payment and give up your right to sue Ideal Image for these claims. Filing takes a few minutes — it's worth it.
