If you used Legacy Health's patient portal between 2019 and 2024, you may be entitled to compensation from a class action settlement.

What happened: Legacy Health allegedly embedded tracking tools from Meta (Facebook) and Google on its patient portal website, which shared your protected health information with these tech companies without your permission.

Here's what you need to know about eligibility, payment amounts, and how to file your claim.

Quick Summary

  • Who's eligible: Legacy Health patients who registered for or logged into the patient portal between Feb 2019 - Feb 2024
  • Where: Oregon and Washington (Legacy Health service areas)
  • Cash payment: $15 per eligible class member
  • Free service: 1 year Medical Shield Complete (worth ~$374)
  • Total settlement: $2.2 million
  • Class members: Approximately 1.2 million people
  • Claim deadline: March 16, 2026
  • Official site: PixelLegacyHealthSettlement.com

Important: This settlement covers patient privacy violations related to website tracking technologies (Meta Pixel and Google Analytics). It does not involve a data breach or unauthorized access to Legacy Health's medical record systems.

What Is Legacy Health?

Legacy Health is a nonprofit healthcare system serving Oregon and Southwest Washington. The organization operates:

  • Seven hospitals
  • Over 90 clinics and medical offices
  • Multiple specialty care centers
  • Patient portal system (MyHealth Online)

Legacy Health's service area primarily covers the Portland metropolitan area and surrounding communities in Oregon and Vancouver, Washington.

What Were the Allegations Against Legacy Health?

A class action lawsuit filed by Katherine Layman alleged that Legacy Health embedded third-party tracking tools on its patient portal website without patient knowledge or consent.

The tracking tools in question:

  • Meta Pixel (formerly Facebook Pixel) - tracking code from Meta/Facebook
  • Google Analytics - website analytics tool from Google

What information was allegedly shared:

  • Patient portal login activity
  • Pages viewed within the patient portal
  • Medical conditions searched or viewed
  • Appointment scheduling information
  • Prescription refill requests
  • Other patient portal interactions

The lawsuit claimed this information could be linked to patients' Facebook or Google accounts, allowing these companies to identify specific individuals and their health-related activities.

Legal claims alleged:

  1. Breach of confidence
  2. Breach of implied contract
  3. Unjust enrichment
  4. Negligence
  5. Violation of Electronic Communications Privacy Act

Legacy Health's response: Legacy Health denies all allegations and any wrongdoing. The settlement is not an admission of liability. Legacy agreed to settle to avoid the cost and uncertainty of continued litigation.

Am I Eligible for the Settlement?

You're eligible if you fall into either of these categories:

Category 1:

  • You received an invitation to register for a Legacy Health patient portal account
  • You registered for the account
  • Registration occurred between February 18, 2019 and December 31, 2020

Category 2:

  • You do not fall into Category 1, BUT
  • You logged into Legacy Health's patient portal at least once
  • Login occurred between January 1, 2021 and February 9, 2024

Additional requirement: You must be a U.S. resident.

How Do I Know If I'm in the Settlement Class?

If you received a notice email or letter from the settlement administrator, you're in the class.

The notice would have been sent to the email address or mailing address Legacy Health had on file for you.

Didn't receive a notice? You may still be eligible if you meet the criteria above. Contact the settlement administrator to verify your eligibility.

What If I Don't Want to Participate?

You have two options if you don't want to be part of this settlement:

Option 1: Opt Out (Exclude Yourself)

What this means:

  • You will not receive any benefits from the settlement
  • You keep your right to sue Legacy Health independently on these claims
  • You can pursue your own lawsuit or join a different one

How to opt out:

  1. Send a written request for exclusion
  2. Include: your full name, address, phone number, and statement requesting exclusion from the settlement
  3. Mail to the settlement administrator address above
  4. Must be postmarked by March 16, 2026

Option 2: Object to the Settlement

What this means:

  • You remain in the settlement class
  • You can still file a claim and receive benefits
  • You tell the court why you think the settlement is unfair or inadequate

How to object:

  1. Write a letter explaining your objection
  2. Include specific reasons why you believe the settlement is unfair
  3. Include your name, address, phone number, and case details
  4. Mail to the settlement administrator
  5. Must be postmarked by March 16, 2026

You may also attend the final approval hearing on April 16, 2026 to voice your objection in person.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will I actually receive?

You can receive a $15 cash payment if you file a claim, plus one year of Medical Shield Complete services (worth approximately $374). The exact cash payment amount depends on the total number of valid claims submitted.

Do I need to live in Oregon or Washington?

You must be a U.S. resident, but Legacy Health primarily operates in Oregon and Vancouver, Washington. If you were a patient who used their patient portal, you likely lived in or near these areas.

Is this settlement legitimate?

Yes. This is a court-approved class action settlement pending final approval. The case is Katherine Layman v. Legacy Health, Case No. 25CV40104, in the Circuit Court of Multnomah County, Oregon. Always verify you're visiting the official website: PixelLegacyHealthSettlement.com

Was my actual medical data stolen or leaked?

No. This case involves tracking pixels that monitored your activity on the patient portal website, not unauthorized access to Legacy Health's medical records database. Your actual medical records stored in Legacy's systems were not compromised.

Do I need to provide proof of anything?

No. If you're in the settlement class, you don't need to provide proof of purchase, receipts, or documentation. You just need to submit a claim form with your Unique ID and PIN.

Will filing a claim affect my current healthcare?

No. Participating in this settlement will not affect your current or future healthcare with Legacy Health or any other provider.

I moved since 2024. Can I still file?

Yes. You can update your address when filing your claim to ensure you receive payment at your current address.

What happened to the lawsuit?

The parties agreed to settle the case. Legacy Health has not admitted any wrongdoing. The court granted preliminary approval in December 2025 and will consider final approval on April 16, 2026.

Should You File a Claim?

Our recommendation: Yes, especially for the Medical Shield Complete service.

Reasons to participate:

  • Quick process: Filing takes approximately 5-10 minutes
  • No cost: Filing a claim is completely free
  • Valuable privacy protection: The Medical Shield Complete service alone is worth approximately $374
  • Cash payment: Even $15 is better than nothing
  • No downside: You give up nothing by participating except the theoretical right to sue Legacy Health on these specific claims

The Medical Shield Complete service is particularly valuable if:

  • Your patient portal activity included sensitive health information

Understanding the Settlement Amount

The total settlement fund is $2.2 million, which will be distributed as follows:

  • Court-approved attorneys' fees and costs
  • Settlement administration expenses
  • $2,500 service award to the class representative (Katherine Layman)
  • Medical Shield Complete services for all class members
  • Cash payments to class members who file claims

With approximately 1.2 million class members, the $15 per person payment is the maximum available after other expenses are paid.

What About Future Privacy Protections?

As part of the settlement, Legacy Health has agreed to enhanced data privacy practices, though specific details are outlined in the settlement agreement.

This settlement is part of a larger trend of healthcare providers being held accountable for using third-party tracking tools that may share protected health information without proper consent.
