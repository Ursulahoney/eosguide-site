Tinder Age Discrimination Settlement – Older California Users May Get a Cash Payment

Tinder agreed to a $60.5 million settlement over allegations it charged older users more for Tinder Plus and Tinder Gold subscriptions than younger users — just for being older. If you were over 28 or 29 and purchased a Tinder subscription in California between 2015 and 2019, you may be eligible for a cash payment. No formal claim form required — but choose your payment method by August 18, 2026 to get paid faster.

What happened

Class period: March 2, 2015 through February 10, 2019

  • Tinder allegedly charged users over the age of 28 or 29 higher prices for Tinder Plus and Tinder Gold subscriptions than younger users — for no reason other than their age
  • This allegedly violated California's Unruh Civil Rights Act, which prohibits age-based price discrimination
  • The case is Candelore v. Tinder, Inc., filed in California
  • Tinder agreed to pay $60.5 million to resolve the claims without admitting liability

Who may qualify

  • You purchased Tinder Plus or Tinder Gold in California
  • You were over the age of 29 at any time between March 2, 2015 and February 10, 2019
  • OR you were over the age of 28 at any time on or after March 2, 2016 through February 10, 2019

How to file

  1. If you received a notice by email, text, or postcard: visit tindercalclassaction.com and choose your payment method (PayPal, Venmo, Zelle, ACH, or mailed check) by August 18, 2026
  2. If you do nothing: the Settlement Administrator will still try to pay you using Tinder's records — but you may receive payment more slowly or to an outdated address
  3. If you did NOT receive a notice but believe you qualify: complete the Verification Form at tindercalclassaction.com by August 18, 2026

Payment timing

Payment timing has not been announced. Payments will be distributed after the court grants final approval at the May 20, 2026 hearing. Choosing your payment method on the settlement website by August 18, 2026 ensures you are paid more quickly and that payment reaches you.

FAQ

Do I need to file a claim?
Not exactly. If you received a notice, just visit tindercalclassaction.com and choose how you want to be paid. If you do nothing, the administrator will still try to pay you using Tinder's records — but you may receive payment more slowly.
Who qualifies?
California residents who purchased Tinder Plus or Tinder Gold and were over the age of 29 between March 2, 2015 and February 10, 2019 (or over 28 on or after March 2, 2016 through February 10, 2019).
What if I never got a notice?
If you believe you qualify but did not receive a notice by email, text, or postcard, complete the Verification Form at tindercalclassaction.com by August 18, 2026.
How do I get paid?
Visit tindercalclassaction.com and choose your preferred payment method — PayPal, Venmo, Zelle, ACH direct deposit, or a mailed check.
What happens if I do nothing?
You remain in the class and the Settlement Administrator will attempt to pay you using your existing Tinder account information. However, you will receive payment faster and more reliably if you choose your method on the website.
What is the April 8 deadline for?
That is the deadline to opt out of the settlement (if you want to keep the right to sue Tinder separately) or to file a written objection. It has nothing to do with receiving your payment.
When will payments be sent?
After the final approval hearing on May 20, 2026, assuming no appeals delay things. Exact payment dates have not been announced — check tindercalclassaction.com for updates.
