If you were a patient at Northwell Health between 2020 and 2024, you may be entitled to compensation from a class action settlement.

What happened: Northwell Health allegedly embedded Meta Pixel and Google Analytics tracking tools on its website and patient portal, which shared your protected health information with Meta (Facebook) and Google without your permission.

Here's what you need to know about eligibility, payment amounts, and how to file your claim.

Quick Summary

  • Where: New York (Northwell Health service areas - Long Island, NYC, Westchester)

Important: This settlement covers patient privacy violations related to website tracking technologies (Meta Pixel and Google Analytics). It does not involve a data breach or unauthorized access to Northwell Health's medical record systems.

What Is Northwell Health?

Northwell Health is New York's largest healthcare provider and private employer, serving the New York metropolitan area. The organization operates:

  • 21 hospitals
  • Over 900 outpatient facilities
  • Multiple specialty care centers
  • FollowMyHealth patient portal system
  • Primary service areas: Long Island, New York City, and Westchester County

Northwell Health serves millions of patients annually across the New York region.

What Were the Allegations Against Northwell Health?

A class action lawsuit filed as Kaplan v. Northwell Health, Inc. alleged that Northwell Health embedded third-party tracking tools on its website and patient portal without patient knowledge or consent.

The tracking tools in question:

  • Meta Pixel (formerly Facebook Pixel) - tracking code from Meta/Facebook
  • Google Analytics - website analytics tool from Google

What information was allegedly shared:

  • Patient portal login activity (FollowMyHealth portal)
  • Pages viewed within the patient portal
  • Appointment booking information on northwell.edu
  • Medical conditions searched or viewed
  • Health-related website interactions
  • Personal identifiable information

The lawsuit claimed this information could be linked to patients' Facebook or Google accounts, allowing these companies to identify specific individuals and their health-related activities.

Legal claims alleged:

  1. Violation of Electronic Communications Privacy Act
  2. Breach of confidence
  3. Breach of implied contract
  4. Unjust enrichment
  5. Negligence
  6. Violation of New York privacy laws

Northwell Health's response: Northwell Health denies all allegations and any wrongdoing. The settlement is not an admission of liability. Northwell agreed to settle to avoid the cost and uncertainty of continued litigation.

Am I Eligible for the Settlement?

You're eligible if you fall into either of these categories:

Settlement Subclass 1:

  • You logged into Northwell's FollowMyHealth patient portal at least once between January 1, 2020 and December 31, 2023, OR
  • You booked an appointment on northwell.edu between January 1, 2020 and December 31, 2023

Settlement Subclass 2:

  • You do not fall into Subclass 1, BUT
  • You were a patient of Northwell Health at any time between January 1, 2020 and July 25, 2024

Excluded from the settlement:

  • Northwell's officers and directors
  • Northwell's legal counsel
  • Members of the judiciary assigned to the case and their families

How Do I Know If I'm in the Settlement Class?

If you received a notice email from the settlement administrator, you're in the class.

The notice would have been sent to the email address or mailing address Northwell Health had on file for you.

Didn't receive a notice? You may still be eligible if you meet the criteria above. Contact the settlement administrator at (833) 360-6887 or info@NWPixelSettlement.com to verify your eligibility.

What If I Don't Want to Participate?

You have two options if you don't want to be part of this settlement:

Option 1: Opt Out (Exclude Yourself)

What this means:

  • You will not receive any benefits from the settlement
  • You keep your right to sue Northwell Health independently on these claims
  • You can pursue your own lawsuit or join a different one

How to opt out:

Send a written exclusion request that must:

  1. Contain the statement: "I unequivocally wish to be excluded from the Settlement Class in the case of Kaplan v. Northwell Health, Inc., Case No. 520763/2025"
  2. Include your full name, address, phone number, and signature
  3. Be postmarked no later than March 23, 2026
  4. Be mailed to the settlement administrator address above

Note: Any request purporting to exclude more than one Settlement Class Member will be deemed invalid.

Option 2: Object to the Settlement

What this means:

  • You remain in the settlement class
  • You can still file a claim and receive benefits
  • You tell the court why you think the settlement is unfair or inadequate

How to object:

No later than March 23, 2026, you must:

  1. File your written objection with the Clerk of Court
  2. Include specific reasons why you believe the settlement is unfair
  3. Include any supporting documentation
  4. Send copies to both Class Counsel and Northwell Health's Counsel (addresses available on settlement website)

You may also attend the final approval hearing on April 21, 2026 at 9:30 AM ET to voice your objection in person.

If you opt out, you cannot object. If your objection is overruled, you remain bound by the settlement terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will I actually receive?

If you're in Subclass 1, you can receive a $15 cash payment plus 12 months of CyEx Privacy Shield Pro privacy monitoring. If you're in Subclass 2, you can receive 12 months of privacy monitoring only. The exact cash payment amount depends on the total number of valid claims submitted.

How do I know which subclass I'm in?

If you logged into the FollowMyHealth patient portal OR booked an appointment on northwell.edu between January 1, 2020 and December 31, 2023, you're in Subclass 1 (eligible for $15 + privacy monitoring). If you were a Northwell patient during 2020-2024 but didn't do those specific actions, you're in Subclass 2 (eligible for privacy monitoring only).

What is CyEx Privacy Shield Pro?

It's a comprehensive privacy protection service that includes a VPN for secure browsing, private search engine, dark web monitoring, password manager, encrypted document vault, and automatic requests to data brokers to remove your personal information.

Is this settlement legitimate?

Yes. This is a court-approved class action settlement pending final approval. The case is Kaplan v. Northwell Health, Inc., Case No. 520763/2025, in the Supreme Court of the State of New York, County of Kings. Always verify you're visiting the official website: NWPixelSettlement.com

Was my actual medical data stolen or leaked?

No. This case involves tracking pixels that monitored your activity on Northwell's website and patient portal, not unauthorized access to Northwell Health's medical records database. Your actual medical records stored in Northwell's systems were not compromised.

Do I need to provide proof of anything?

No. If you're in the settlement class, you don't need to provide proof of purchase, receipts, medical records, or documentation. You just need to submit a claim form with your Notice ID and PIN.

Will filing a claim affect my current healthcare?

No. Participating in this settlement will not affect your current or future healthcare with Northwell Health or any other provider.

I'm a current Northwell patient. Can I still file?

Yes! Being a current patient does not disqualify you. As long as you meet the eligibility criteria for the relevant time periods, you can file a claim.
