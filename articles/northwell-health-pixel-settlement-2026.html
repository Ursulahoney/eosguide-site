If you were a patient at Northwell Health between 2020 and 2024, you may be entitled to compensation from a class action settlement.

What happened: Northwell Health allegedly embedded Meta Pixel and Google Analytics tracking tools on its website and patient portal, which shared your protected health information with Meta (Facebook) and Google without your permission.

Here's what you need to know about eligibility, payment amounts, and how to file your claim.

Quick Summary

  • Who's eligible: Northwell Health patients who logged into FollowMyHealth portal or booked appointments between 2020-2024
  • Where: New York (Northwell Health service areas - Long Island, NYC, Westchester)
  • Cash payment: $15 for Subclass 1 members
  • Free service: 12 months CyEx Privacy Shield Pro (includes VPN, dark web monitoring, password manager)
  • Total eligible: Approximately hundreds of thousands of patients
  • Claim deadline: April 20, 2026
  • Official site: NWPixelSettlement.com

Important: This settlement covers patient privacy violations related to website tracking technologies (Meta Pixel and Google Analytics). It does not involve a data breach or unauthorized access to Northwell Health's medical record systems.

What Is Northwell Health?

Northwell Health is New York's largest healthcare provider and private employer, serving the New York metropolitan area. The organization operates:

  • 21 hospitals
  • Over 900 outpatient facilities
  • Multiple specialty care centers
  • FollowMyHealth patient portal system
  • Primary service areas: Long Island, New York City, and Westchester County

Northwell Health serves millions of patients annually across the New York region.

What Were the Allegations Against Northwell Health?

A class action lawsuit filed as Kaplan v. Northwell Health, Inc. alleged that Northwell Health embedded third-party tracking tools on its website and patient portal without patient knowledge or consent.

The tracking tools in question:

  • Meta Pixel (formerly Facebook Pixel) - tracking code from Meta/Facebook
  • Google Analytics - website analytics tool from Google

What information was allegedly shared:

  • Patient portal login activity (FollowMyHealth portal)
  • Pages viewed within the patient portal
  • Appointment booking information on northwell.edu
  • Medical conditions searched or viewed
  • Health-related website interactions
  • Personal identifiable information

The lawsuit claimed this information could be linked to patients' Facebook or Google accounts, allowing these companies to identify specific individuals and their health-related activities.

Legal claims alleged:

  1. Violation of Electronic Communications Privacy Act
  2. Breach of confidence
  3. Breach of implied contract
  4. Unjust enrichment
  5. Negligence
  6. Violation of New York privacy laws

Northwell Health's response: Northwell Health denies all allegations and any wrongdoing. The settlement is not an admission of liability. Northwell agreed to settle to avoid the cost and uncertainty of continued litigation.

Am I Eligible for the Settlement?

You're eligible if you fall into either of these categories:

Settlement Subclass 1:

  • You logged into Northwell's FollowMyHealth patient portal at least once between January 1, 2020 and December 31, 2023, OR
  • You booked an appointment on northwell.edu between January 1, 2020 and December 31, 2023

Settlement Subclass 2:

  • You do not fall into Subclass 1, BUT
  • You were a patient of Northwell Health at any time between January 1, 2020 and July 25, 2024

Excluded from the settlement:
- Northwell's officers and directors
- Northwell's legal counsel
- Members of the judiciary assigned to the case and their families
